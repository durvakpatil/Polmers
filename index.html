Electronic Materials

Conducting Polymers

Organic polymers that can conduct electricity. They possess a conjugated backbone (alternating single and double bonds) which allows electrons to move freely.

Doping Mechanism in Polyacetylene

Conductivity of pure acetylene is about 4.4 × 10⁻⁵ S/cm. Upon doping with oxidizing agents like iodine, the conductivity increases dramatically to about 400 S/cm.

  • Before Doping: Insulator with a band gap due to Peierls Distortion (alternating bonds). Electrons are locked.
  • P-Type (Oxidation): Iodine (I₂) removes an electron from the backbone, creating a moving positive charge (polaron/hole).
  • N-Type (Reduction): Sodium (Na) donates an electron to the backbone, creating a moving negative charge.
  • After Effect: Dopant ions stabilize charges, bonds equalize, enabling massive conductivity (Synthetic Metal).

Classification of Conducting Polymer

Extrinsically Conducting Polymer

Prepared by mixing insulating polymers with conducting fillers such as:

  • Metal fibers
  • Carbon black
  • Metal oxides

These fillers create conductive pathways inside the polymer matrix, enabling electrical conduction.

FILLER 0%

Intrinsically Conducting Polymer

Possess electrical conductivity due to their own molecular structure. Conductivity arises because of:

  • Conjugation
  • Planarity
  • Alternating single and double bonds

This arrangement allows electrons to move freely along the polymer chain.

High Electrical Conductivity — Charge Storage Material

Polyacetylene becomes highly conductive after doping because electrons become mobile along its conjugated polymer backbone.

Due to high electrical conductivity, lightweight structure, and fast electron transfer, it acts as a promising material for charge storage systems such as rechargeable batteries and supercapacitors.

Static electricity buildup can create dangerous sparks in explosive environments (like fuel tanks and chemical plants).

Coating insulating materials with conducting polymers like polyacetylene helps safely dissipate electrical charges before a spark can form.

Optical Materials

Electroluminescent Polymers

Polymers that emit light when an electric current passes through them. They are the core technology behind flexible OLED displays.

Construction and Working of OLED

  • Stack Construction: The OLED consists of a transparent glass substrate, an ITO anode, an emissive polymer layer, and a metallic cathode.
  • Charge Injection: Upon applying voltage, electrons are injected from the cathode and holes from the anode into the polymer.
  • Exciton Recombination: The opposite charges attract, form an exciton, and collapse, releasing energy.
  • Photon Emission: The released energy emits a bright beam of visible light through the transparent substrate.

1. Diamagnetic Material

PPV weakly repels magnetic fields because all electrons are paired. Watch the field lines bend.
